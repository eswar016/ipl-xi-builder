# IPL XI Builder 🏆

A sleek, responsive, and fully interactive **IPL Playing XI Builder** designed specifically for 2026 IPL fans and content creators. Build your ultimate cricket lineups effortlessly combining a massive local database, dynamic drag-and-drop interactions, and deep customization features.

## 🚀 Features

- **Full 10-Team Database**: All 10 IPL franchises fully embedded out of the box with actual 2026 player rosters, captaincy tags, and overseas status.
- **Drag & Drop UI**: Intuitively construct your playing XI natively just by dragging players from your filtered squad onto an interactive green-pitch canvas.
- **Smart Grouping**: Players are automatically sorted into clean role categories (Batters, All-Rounders, Wicketkeepers, Bowlers) for effortless searching.
- **Stat Validations**: Live counters track your 11-player limit and enforce the official 4-overseas player maximum limit.
- **Split-Screen Compare Mode**: Instantly freeze and lock your active XI to the right-hand panel, then build an entirely different team on the left exactly side-by-side to compare head-to-head.
- **Persistent Local Storage**: Built a legendary lineup? Click "Save" and your masterpiece is securely locked into your browser cache, waiting to be "Loaded" back the next time you open the app.
- **Dynamic Colored Themes**: The entire app environment dynamically shifts its accents, borders, and UI glow to match the exact HEX colors of whatever franchise you are actively commanding.

## 🛠️ Technology Stack

- **HTML5**: semantic structural base with FontAwesome icon integration.
- **CSS3**: Extensive use of variables, flex/grid architectures, transition effects, blurring, and pseudo-element masking for that premium aesthetic.
- **Vanilla JavaScript**: Zero frameworks. Hand-coded drag-and-drop state lifecycle, `localStorage` manipulation, and DOM injection. 

## 🏗️ Getting Started

1. Clone or download the repository to your local machine.
2. Ensure all assets (including PNG role icons) are stored inside your respective folders.
3. Open `index.html` in any modern web browser.
4. Play!
